Queensland Rehabilitation Principles of Care

Developed through thematic review of NSW, Victoria, Scotland, Netherlands, WHO and NHS England frameworks, and feedback from the August 2025 Qld Rehab Clinical Network Forum.

Principle 1
Person-centred and goal-based rehabilitation
Rehabilitation should be tailored to the individual across their lifespan including consideration of their goals, values, and context, involving carers and families in a respectful partnership.
Shared decision-makingPerson-centred goal settingCulturally & linguistically safePaediatric transitionDevelopmentally appropriate
Principle 2
Holistic and inclusive care
Rehabilitation should take a holistic and individualised approach that addresses the unique aspects of a person, including their physical, psychological, cognitive-communication, cultural and social needs.
Trauma-informed careSelf-managementHealth literacyCarer inclusionCulturally safe care
Principle 3
Equitable and timely access to rehabilitation
Rehabilitation services must be accessible, equitable, delivered in a timely and appropriate manner. Services should be available across the state and close to home including rural and remote Queensland.
Value-based careEarly rehabilitationIn-reachTelehealth & outreachRural & remoteScreening tools
Principle 4
Integrated and coordinated service delivery
Rehabilitation should be integrated across the continuum of care, coordinated across settings and teams, flexible to changing needs, and foster collaboration across sectors and disciplines.
Patient flowClinical services planningInterdisciplinary carePublic/private collaboration
Principle 5
Sustainable and specialised workforce and expertise
A skilled, adaptable, and well-supported interdisciplinary workforce is essential for high-quality rehabilitation. This requires adequate resourcing, clear alignment of tasks and skills, proactive workforce planning, and recognition of rehabilitation as a specialist field.
ANSNAP V5 & AFRM standardsMultidisciplinary teamsAdvanced scope rolesProfessional development
Principle 6
Use of evidence, data, and evaluation
Rehabilitation should be guided by evidence, informed by data, and continuously evaluated for improvement.
AROC FIM/WeeFIMOutcome measuresAccreditationContinuous improvementBenchmarking
Principle 7
Innovation and technology
Embracing innovation and digital tools enhances access, efficiency, and patient engagement.
Tele-rehabilitationDigital equityCo-designCyber securityEthical use of technology

Scope of the QLD Rehab MOC

In scope ✓
✓
Adult multidisciplinary rehabilitation services — levels 2–6 of the Qld Health Clinical Services Capability Framework
Delivered across all Queensland HHS
✓
In-reach to acute setting
✓
Sub-acute inpatient rehabilitation services
✓
Ambulatory, home-based, outreach and day hospital rehabilitation services
Provided under an admitted or non-admitted (Tier 2) model

Transition of adolescent and young adult from paediatric services
✓
Paediatric rehabilitation services
✓
Telehealth and virtual care
✓
Linkage to statewide service models

✓
Single-discipline rehabilitation programs

✗
Mental health rehabilitation services (separate workstream)
✗
NDIS-funded community services
✗
Private providers (noting aspects of MOC may apply)
✗
Cardiac and pulmonary rehabilitation
✗
Drug and alcohol rehabilitation services

Transitional Care Program (TCP) short-term restorative care

Implementation plan — QLD Rehabilitation MOC
Three-phase approach adapted from ACI Implementation Framework · Expand each phase below
1
Phase 1 — Plan
Active · Apr–Jun 2025
A
Establish executive sponsorship & governance
Identify executive sponsor (ideally HHS or Queensland Health executive). Establish Clinical Advisory Group with Network Executive. Define steering committee with Aboriginal Health and consumer representation.
B
Identify project lead & team
Appoint dedicated project lead for the implementation period. Establish working groups for key deliverables. Include heads of department, senior allied health, nursing, and clinical services planning representatives.
C
Define goals, objectives & scope
Agree on shared vision statement. Document project scope, objectives, timelines, stakeholder map, communications plan and risk log. Confirm in/out of scope Thursday 3 April.
D
Stakeholder engagement plan
Map all Queensland HHS rehabilitation leads. Identify consumer and carer representatives. Plan for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander community co-design. Engage Primary Health Networks and ACCHS partners.
Phase 1 checklist
2
Phase 2 — Assess
Planned · Jun–Oct 2025
A
Current state assessment
Review existing Queensland rehabilitation services across all HHS. Map current care settings, referral pathways and patient flows. Analyse AROC data, service activity data and patient outcomes. Review workforce distribution and gaps across Queensland.
B
Identify gaps & priorities
Use the Rehabilitation Service Delivery Audit Tool (ACI 2023) adapted for Queensland context. Identify HHS-level gaps in care settings, workforce, and access. Map disparities in rural, regional and remote service availability.
C
Evidence review
Review peer-reviewed literature and grey literature for Queensland-specific context. Reference NSW, Victorian and international MOC evidence reports. Triangulate empirical data with stakeholder consultation findings.
D
Consumer & community consultation
Conduct consumer surveys and lived experience interviews. Hold community forums including ATSI community representatives. Engage carers and families across urban and regional Queensland.
Phase 2 checklist
3
Phase 3 — Develop & consult
Planned · Oct 2025–Mar 2026
A
Draft MOC development
Develop each section of the MOC document: principles, care settings, workforce, referral pathways, data and outcomes, implementation guidance. Include Queensland-specific case studies for each care setting.
B
Statewide consultation
Distribute draft MOC to all Queensland HHS for formal consultation. Hold targeted consultation sessions with specialty groups (brain injury, spinal cord injury, paediatrics). Seek feedback from primary care, ACCHS and private sector partners.
C
Incorporate feedback & peer review
Collate and analyse all consultation feedback. Revise draft MOC accordingly. Commission independent peer review panel. Present revised draft to steering committee for endorsement.
Phase 3 checklist
4
Phase 4 — Finalise & launch
Planned · Apr–Sep 2026
A
Final approvals & publication
Obtain executive endorsement and Queensland Health approval. Finalise design and layout. Publish MOC on Queensland Health website with supporting resources and implementation toolkit.
B
Launch & communications
Coordinate statewide launch event. Develop communication strategy for clinicians, health managers, executives and consumers. Present at relevant Queensland conferences and network forums.
C
Evaluation & monitoring framework
Establish key performance indicators and baseline data. Develop evaluation plan with 2-yearly review cycle. Set up Community of Practice to support local implementation across HHS. Identify quick wins for early implementation.
